About

Still early. Already building.

I'm a first-year Computer Science Engineering student at Government Engineering College, Palamu which means I'm still early in the formal part of this, and deliberately impatient about the rest. Instead of waiting for a syllabus to tell me when I'm ready, I've been shipping small products, taking on freelance work, and contributing wherever open source will have me.

What pulls me toward web development is the immediacy of it write something, refresh a browser, and watch an idea turn into an interface someone can actually use. I built FocusFlows because I wanted a productivity tool that felt as calm as the focus it's supposed to protect, and that instinct design first, then engineer to match it is how I approach most things I build now.

Internships, hackathons, and campus ambassador programs have been my classroom outside the classroom. Each one has been less about the certificate and more about getting comfortable shipping under real constraints, with real feedback, on someone else's timeline.
